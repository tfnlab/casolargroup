Energy efficiency ratings

Energy raters can provide ratings for residential and commercial buildings to determine their energy efficiency. These ratings can help building owners and managers identify areas where energy is being wasted and recommend ways to improve efficiency.

Energy audits

Energy raters can perform energy audits to identify areas where buildings are losing energy, such as through air leaks or insufficient insulation. They can then recommend improvements to reduce energy consumption and save money.

HERS ratings

Home Energy Rating System (HERS) ratings are used to certify homes for energy efficiency. Energy raters can perform HERS ratings to help homeowners understand how much energy their homes are using and identify ways to improve efficiency.

Energy code compliance testing

Energy raters can perform energy code compliance testing to ensure that buildings meet local and national energy codes. This can help building owners and managers avoid costly fines and penalties for non-compliance.

Indoor air quality testing

Energy raters can perform indoor air quality testing to assess the air quality inside buildings and recommend improvements to reduce pollutants and improve health.

Green building certifications

Energy raters can assist with obtaining green building certifications such as LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) and Energy Star certifications. These certifications demonstrate a commitment to energy efficiency and sustainability.
